How to Teach-A-Robot

For each goal, first select a good grasp so that the robot knows how to hold the object. Click and drag the colored rings to change the orientation of the hand.

When you've chosen a good grasp, click on the "Accept Grasp" button.

To move the robot towards the goal, click and drag on the green marker at its base.

To move the gripper of the robot, click and drag the green marker.

Press the z-key while dragging the green marker to move the gripper up and down.

For more control over the gripper orientation and the elbow, select the "Advanced Gripper Controls" button.

How do I move the hand?

To move the hand, simply click on the green marker and drag it to the desired destination. You can move the hand up and down by holding the Z key while you drag.

How do I change the orientation of the hand?

To change the orientation of the hand, first press the Full Control button. Then, use the colored rings that appear around the hand to change it's roll, pitch, and yaw.

How do I move the elbow?

To move the elbow, first press the Full Control button. Then, use the colored ring around the upper arm to roll the upper arm, moving the elbow to the desired position.

How do I choose a grasp?

Before each goal, you must first choose how you would like the robot to grasp the object. To do this, move the red hand marker to the desired grasp, using the same controls as for moving the hand of the robot. Then, press Accept Grasp.

How do I move the base?

To move the base, simply click on the green marker at the robot's base, and drag it to the desired position. To rotate the base, click and drag the blue ring around the base.
